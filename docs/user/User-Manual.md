# User Manual

## Introduction

MRRG-Mobile allows field workers to access assigned jobs, report progress and communicate with the office directly from the job site.

The application is designed to remain simple to use while supporting work in areas with limited network connectivity.

The screenshots shown throughout this guide illustrate the current version of the application. Minor interface changes may occur over time while the underlying workflows remain unchanged.

---

## Logging In

Accounts are created by an administrator.

Before the first login, new users must activate their account using the activation link received by email.

After activation, the application can be accessed using the assigned email address and chosen password.

## Dashboard

The dashboard displays assigned work grouped by day, allowing workers to quickly review their upcoming schedule.

Jobs are grouped by day and include the information required before arriving on site, including:

- customer details;
- address;
- scheduled start time;
- job status;
- notes when available.

Workers can switch between the 3-day and weekly views depending on their planning needs.

Pulling down on the dashboard refreshes job information and updates the application when a network connection is available.

## Completing Work

Each assigned job follows a predefined workflow.

```text
Assigned

   ↓

Take BEFORE Photo

   ↓

In Progress

   ↓

Take AFTER Photo

   ↓

Submit

   ↓

Waiting Manager Validation

   ↓  

Completed
```

Workers begin the job by uploading at least one before photo, which automatically moves the job to In Progress.

During the work they may add notes whenever necessary. Notes provide additional information but do not affect the job status.

Once the work has been completed, workers upload at least one after photo before submitting the job for manager validation.

## Before and After Photos

Photographs provide a visual record of both the work site's initial condition and the completed work.

Before photos document the initial condition of the work site and must be captured before work begins. Uploading the first before photo automatically starts the job.

After photos document the completed work and are required before the job can be submitted for manager validation. After photos cannot be uploaded until at least one before photo has been added.

These photographs are attached to the job and reviewed by the manager during the validation process.

Clear photographs help managers validate completed work more efficiently.

## Working Offline

MRRG-Mobile continues to operate even when network connectivity is unavailable.

Previously downloaded jobs remain accessible even without an internet connection.

Once connectivity is restored, pending work is automatically sent to the office.

The application indicates when displayed information may not be up to date because the device is offline.

---

## Notifications

The application notifies workers when:

- new jobs are assigned;
- schedules change;
- important updates are available.

Notifications remain available in the notification centre, allowing workers to review previous updates even after they have been opened or dismissed from the device.

## Good Practices

For the best experience:

- synchronize regularly when an internet connection is available;
- review job information before travelling to site;
- start each job by capturing at least one before photo;
- capture clear before and after photographs;
- upload at least one after photo before submitting a job for validation;
- submit completed work promptly;
- keep notifications enabled to receive important updates;
- keep the application up to date;
